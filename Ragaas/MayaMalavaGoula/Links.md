Mayatheetha Swaroopini was composed by Ponnayya Pillai. 
Specifically, it was composed by the first Ponnayya Pillai (1804–1864), the second of the four famous brothers known as the Thanjavur Quartet. 
Key details regarding this composition include:
Tribute to a Guru: The song is part of a set of nine kritis composed by the brothers to honor their guru, Muthuswami Dikshitar.
Signature Lyrics: It contains the phrase "maa Guruguha swamiki ne dasudaithi" (I am a servant to the master Guruguha), using the mudra (signature) of Dikshitar as a mark of respect.
Musical Structure:
Raga: Mayamalavagowla.
Tala: Roopaka.
Language: Telugu.
The Deity: The song is a prayer to the goddess Brihadeeswari of the Thanjavur Brihadeeswarar Temple.
